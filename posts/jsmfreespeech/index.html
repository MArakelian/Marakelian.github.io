John Stuart Mill on Free Speech

Jun 9 2021 07:00 PM EDT
Political Philosophy , Philosophy of Law

On the next stream we’re taking a look at John Stuart Mill’s arguments for freedom of speech in chapter 2 of On Liberty: On the Liberty of Thought and Discussion. In particular, we’ll take a look at the idea of the marketplace of ideas.

We’re going to cover three different interpretations of Mill’s arguments:

  1. Absolutist: Any interference with an agent’s freedom of speech (properly understood) counts as a violation of liberty rights.

  2. Pragmatic: The regulation of speech falls under the jurisdiction of society, but it turns out that restrictions on speech are never optimal (according to the utilitarian calculus).

  3. Qualified: Mill does not defend freedom of speech at all, but only a freedom of harmless speech.

Mill’s arguments about freedom of speech and thought have been overwhelmingly influential in the United States (and elsewhere), so we’ll go on to take a look at how Mill’s arguments have influenced US jurisprudence.
