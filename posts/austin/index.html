John Austin's Command Theory of Law and Legal Positivsm

Thu May 20 2021 07:00 PM EDT
Philosophy of Law , Jurisprudence

Today’s Question

What are the validity conditions for a law? Under what conditions do laws exist?

Legal Positivism

  • The existence laws depends on social facts and not on the merits of its content.

Austin’s Take

The existence of law is one thing its merit and demerit another. Whether it be or be not is one inquiry; whether it be or be not comfortable to an assumed standard is a different inquiry.

Sovereigns and Commands

  • Austin argues that laws are commands given by a sovereign that are backed by sanctions.

The Province of Jurisprudence Determined (1832)

  • Start with the most general definition of law

A rule laid down for the guidance of an intelligent being by an intelligent being having power over him (L1).

Laws of God and Men

  • There are two species of law: (1) those made by God, and (2) those made by people (L1)

Laws of Men

  • Laws made by people are have two species:

  • (1) Positive Law: “law of position” - i.e. coming from a sovereign with authority by virtue of their power over others. The aggregate of rules coming from political superiors.

  • (2) Positive Morality: conventions established through the opinions of the masses. Fashion, etiquette, etc. (These are not law)

Commands

  • Positive law is law because it results from a command.

  • Commands: an expression of some desire that someone do or refrain from something with a sanction for non compliance

  • Sanctions are necessary for commands, simply asking someone to do or not do something is not a command.

Obligation

  • A sanction binds or obliges one to obey the command.

  • This results in a duty to obey the command.

  • Wherever there is a duty, there has been a command. Wherever there is a command, there is a duty.

Let’s Take Stock

  • Laws are of two sorts: Gods laws and laws made by people.

  • Laws made by people also have two sorts: Positive law (true laws) and positive morality (not really laws).

  • Positive Laws are commands, which means that they are an expression of some desire to do or not do something backed by a sanction (or some punishment) for disobedience.

  • Where there is a command, there is a duty to obey, because the sanction obliges us to follow the law.

What about Rewards?

  • What if some command is backed by some reward to provide an incentive to people? (Tax breaks or Ohio Vaccine Lottery?)

  • These don’t compel performance, so they don’t result in a duty and are not commands. Therefore they’re not laws.

  • Instead, Austin argues that they create an “eventual right”

Types of Commands

  • Laws or Rules: obligate generally to acts or forbearance of a class (Laws)

  • Occasional or particular Rule: obliges to a specific act or forbearance, or to acts or forbearance which it determines specifically or individually (not laws).

Examples of Commands

If Parliament prohibited simply the exportation of corn, either for a given period or indefinitely…[Law or rule]. An order issued by Parliament to meet an impending scarcity, and stopping the exportation of corn then shipped and in port…[occasional rule].

  • Judicial commands are typically occasional.

Who gets to Make the Laws

  • Laws and other commands made by “superiors” and bind “inferiors”.

…Superiority signifies might: the power of affecting others with evil or pain, and of forcing them, through fear of that evil, to fashion their conduct to one’s wishes.

Superiors and Sovereigns

  • There most superior is the sovereign.

  • The “bulk” of the society is in the habit of obedience to a determinate and common superior

  • That superior is not in the habit of obedience to other people.

Utility and the Law

  • Whether a law exists is not a matter of its ethical merit

  • But Austin is a utilitarian (he was influenced by Bentham), so whether we should ultimately follow the law is a matter of utility.

Objections

  • Commands oblige, but do they create obligation? (Hart)

  • Do all laws have sanctions? What about laws conferring authority? (Hart)
